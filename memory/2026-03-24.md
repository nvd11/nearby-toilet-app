
## 2026-03-24 13:14 UTC - RAG Evaluation Pipeline (Phase 4 & DB Design for Phase 5)

*   **Action**: Completed the implementation, testing, and execution of Phase 4 (`InferenceRunner`) for the RAG Evaluator project.
*   **Key Learnings & Decisions**:
    *   **Integration Testing Architecture**: Tests targeting real databases and LLMs must utilize `Targeted Deletion` anchored by dynamic UUIDs (`batch_name` or `run_id`) to ensure isolation during concurrent runs and prevent accidental historical data deletion. We documented these as `DESIGN DECISION 1` and `2` within the pytest suite.
    *   **GFW & Proxy gRPC Blocking**: In mainland China environments, LangChain's asynchronous structured outputs (using `ainvoke` with `.with_structured_output`) default to the gRPC protocol, which typically fails over standard HTTP/1.1 proxies, causing 600-second deadlocks (FD Shutdown on Google IP `142.250.77.10:443`). 
    *   **Proxy Fallback Solution**: Implemented a mandatory timeout (`30s`) in `GeminiLLMFactory` and a synchronous thread-delegation wrapper (`await asyncio.to_thread(self.chain.invoke, ...)`) whenever `ENABLE_PROXY` is toggled. This forces the use of the `requests` library (REST transport), bypassing gRPC entirely. We also injected `GRPC_PROXY` and `grpc_proxy` into the OS environment as a safety net.
    *   **Evaluation Database Design (Phase 5 Prep)**: Designed the `evaluation_results` table. Discarded the idea of dumping all scores blindly by `query_id`. Implemented the `evaluation_strategy` dimension (e.g., `CASE1_GROUND_TRUTH`, `CASE2_RAG_TRIAD`) combined with `judge_model` to form a composite Unique Constraint. This allows a single query to be evaluated across multiple methodologies without metric collision, ensuring clean BI dashboard aggregations.
